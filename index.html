"A City Writ in Art"

Nice is a city located along south coast of France on the French Riviera and has long been a destination for artists and tourists. Bordered by the Alps to the north and the Mediterranean sea to the south, it is a beaufitul town with breathtaking views of the waterfront that have earned the coastline the name "Azure Coast".

An aerial view of the city from over the Bay of Angels. One can see the Azure Coast on the foreground, with the Alps rising in the background.


The sigil of Nice is a crowned red bird standing atop three green hills over the water.



Old Town

The old town section of Nice is home to colorful houses and narrow streets that have for many years been an inspiration for painters. It preserves the rich architectural heritage that the city is famed for and is a sight to behold, attracting tourists from all over the world to experience its sights, sounds and the local cuisines.

Water Attractions

The city of Nice offers breathtaking views of the Azure Coast, with pebbly beaches where tourists flock to in the summer. Further inland there are fountains including the iconic Miroir d'Eau which makes for spectacular viewing especially in spring and in the summer.

Museums

The city of Nice is steeped in centuries of art and culture, from Italian works in the 18th century to modern and contemporary art of the age, and everything in between. It boasts the most museums in a single city in France, which have and continue to preserve said art. The most famous art museums are the Musée Masséna and the Musée Jules Chéret des Beaux-Arts

Fact Check


The city is linked to the rest of France by the French national motorway network and its airport is the second-largest in the country, and it is also connected to the capital, Paris, by high-speed train. This accessibility makes it all the more desirable to tourists, and thus the city is a major economic influence in the region
