"A City Writ in Art"

Nice is a city located along south coast of France on the French Riviera and has long been a destination for artists and tourists. Bordered by the Alps to the north and the Mediterranean sea to the south, it is a beaufitul town with breathtaking views of the waterfront that have earned the coastline the name "Azure Coast".

An aerial view of the city from over the Bay of Angels. One can see the Azure Coast on the foreground, with the Alps rising in the background.


The sigil of Nice is a crowned red bird standing atop three green hills over the water.

Old Town

The very first construction of the current Old Town began all the way back in the 12th century, growing through the centuries and undergoing changes in structure as well as politics, which saw the town annexed by France to become a territory of Italy, and only returned to French administration in the mid 19th century, hence the heavy Italian influence on its architecture and art. The Old Town is home to rows upon rows of variably colorful storied buildings with narrow (some even cobbled) streets that have hundreds of shops, restaurants and galleries where tourists can view and buy pieces of art, appreciate the local cuisine and where locals generally conduct trade.

The beautiful confecture of houses have also been the subject of many an artist's paintings which is one of the main attractions of the so-called Vieux Nice. Presently, it is bordered by Castle Hill to the east, the famed Promenade des Anglais to the south and the Promenade du Paillon, formerly the Paillon River, to the north. The modern city center borders it to the west.

Water Attractions

The city of Nice offers breathtaking views of the Azure Coast, with pebbly beaches where tourists flock to in the summer. The coastline offers breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea over the Bay of Angels, the Promenade de Anglais and the port. The Promenade de Anglais is widely considered the place to be in Nice, built on a 7 km stretch on the waterfront and hosting hotels along the beach. It contains a walkway that has become popular with bikers, roller skaters and runners, a fantastic place for exercise and relaxation on your tour of the city.

Further inland there are fountains including the iconic Miroir d'Eau which makes for spectacular viewing especially in spring and in the summer. The so called Water Mirror was constructed over the River Paillon which was filled up and built over, the area later named the Promenade du Paillon. The springs at the Water Mirror emit water jets up to 5 meters in the air and are a popular with the children, while reflecting the surrounding environs perfectly when the spring is perfectly still. In the mornings they emit a fine mist to imitate the river mist that would have been seen when it flowed through the city.

Museums

The city of Nice is steeped in centuries of art and culture, from Italian works in the 18th century to modern and contemporary art of the age, and everything in between. It boasts the most museums in a single city in France, which have and continue to preserve said art. The most famous art museums are the Musée Masséna and the Musée Jules Chéret des Beaux-Arts. The city is packed with history ranging from the ruins of the Roman Empire to widely acclaimed works from French master artists such as Henri Matisse, as well as insights into the French nobility who lived in the area in the 19th century. Whether you are an art enthusiast or a history major, a fan of the 60's pop culture or you just appreciate culture and architecture, there is something for everyone in this beautiful coastal city.

Fact Check

The city is linked to the rest of France by the French national motorway network and its airport is the second-largest in the country, and it is also connected to the capital, Paris, by high-speed train. This accessibility makes it all the more desirable to tourists, and thus the city is a major economic influence in the region. The city itself is sufficiently accessible via road, bike lanes and city tramways.

As mentioned, the city is home to the second-largest airport in the country, therefore foreign tourists need not go through the capital to access it. Located approximately 7 km from the city center, the Nice Côte d'Azur Airport serves more than just the city of Nice; its proximity to the city of Monaco makes it a critical terminal in the region, and can handle up to 14 million passengers a year, with the exception of the impact of the recent COVID-19 pandemic in recent times having seen its usage drop dramatically. Numbers have fluctuated as the situation worldwide continues to be monitored.

The city owes its Italian art influence to its proximity to the French-Italian border. Tourists looking for a trip out of town can consequently reach Italy or Monaco to either side by helicopter or road, with the Italy border only 20 km away. It makes for as beautiful a hub as one can hope for in the south of France, the Riviera offering miles of beautiful views along the seafront with majestic views of the Alps in the distance. The city of Nice is therefore a popular tourist destination, summer offering the prospect of pebbled beaches and winter offering skiing opportunities up in the mountains.
